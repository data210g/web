In & Beyond the Textbook

Working in data science means keeping up with advancements in the field (and it is a broad field). Here are some key main & supplemental texts we will be using as part of the course (NOTE: the abbreviations between the {} brackets are used in the schedule at the end of the document):

  • ‡Discovering Knowledge in Data, Wiley, 2014 Main text for the course {DKD}
  • ‡An Introduction to Data Science, Stanton, Syracuse University (free/online, also avaiable for free via Apple iBooks) {IDS}
  • †Cookbook for R Chang, RStudio (free/online {CBR})
  • ‡The Split-Apply-Combine Strategy for Data Analysis Wickham, Journal of Statistical Software April 2011, Volume 40, Issue 1. (free/online) {SAC}
  • ‡Tidy Data, Journal of Statistical Software August 2014, Volume 59, Issue 10 (free/online) {TD}
  • ‡XML for Dummies.pdf) (online) {XML}
  • ‡The case for JSON, Batchelder (online) {JSON1}
  • ‡JSON Tutorial (online) {JSON2}
  • ‡Using APIs: Not Quite as Hard as it Looks (online) {API1}
  • ‡APIs for Dummies (online) {API2}
  • ‡Web Scraping in R, Sievert, (online) {SCR1}
  • ‡Web scraping with R & rvest, Machlis (online) {SCR2}
  • ‡CSS Basics (online) {SCR3}
  • ‡Visualizaing Data, Rudis (online) {VIS1}
  • ‡Data visualization basics, de la Cerda (online) {VIS2}
  • ‡R Markdown (online) {RPT1}
